Tracking the Cognitive Journey: From Baseline to Gamified Survey Outcomes

The survey gamification experiment consisted of three tasks conducted two times with a two-week break between each session. The tasks were: cognitively demanding Stroop test, baseline (no engagement in any activity) and filling in four surveys.

Baseline Phase

In the baseline phase, participants were asked to be in a relaxed state for 3 minutes. They were not allowed to perform any tasks to prevent any distractions.

Key insights:

  • BVP: Both post and pre gamified survey started at similar levels of BVP.
  • EDA: Post gamified survey group saw a lower starting level of EDA than the pre gamified survey.

In the cognitive phase, the participants were asked to name the ink color of a word which is the name of the color (most often different color than the ink). This was a cognitively challenging task that requires the participants' full focus to choose the right color.

Key insights:

  • BVP: Compared to the baseline, the BVP taken during the cognitive test is more volatile for both the pre and post survey results.
  • EDA: The post gamificaton survey started at a lower mean EDA value.

The last portion of the study is the survey gamification experiment. This shows the differences in cognitive activity between a gamified survey and the non-gamified survey.

Key insights:

  • BVP: Both post and pre gamified survey saw similar levels of BVP.
  • EDA: Post gamified survey group saw a lower starting level of EDA than the pre gamified survey.
